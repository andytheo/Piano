THE PIANO

What is a Piano?

A piano is a musical instrment - usually seven octaves, consisting of white and black keys - played by pressing the keys. The piano belongs to the percussion family of instruments. Its full name, pianoforte means soft and loud.

Types of Piano

  • Grand piano
  • Upright piano
  • Electronic piano

Grand piano

Grand pianos have a unique sound, touch and feel, they provide dynamics.They are classy and give the best playability. Most people consider it to be the mother of all instruments.

Grand pianos are an absolute beaty to behold. Some non-players even own them because of their aesthetic features, they are always noticed in places.
Grand pianos are the number one instrument played by pianists. Many piano players prefer a particular brand of piano. Some prefer Steinway and Sons, others Yamaha. No matter, the brand one enjoys the rich sound of the piano.
One draw-back of this beauty of an instrument is that it is not easily moved. It requires enough for its transportation.

Upright piano

Upright pianos are smaller in size compared to the Grand piano, therefore they take lesser space. They have a great feel, touch and sound. They are more common in homes. They can be transported with a relative ease compared to the grand piano.

Electronic piano

An electric piano or keyboard produces equivalent sound as a grand piano. However, the sound is not acoustic. They are powered using electricity. These have a very huge advantage having numerous voices, sounds and functions. These features are mostly appreciated by musicians who consider the grand monotonous and prefer other rich sounds such as strings, guitar, violin, drum and a whole lot of other voices.

Producers often use electronic keyboards for their musical arrangements. Beats, tunes, instrumentals consisting of various instruments can be arranged with a single keyboard.
One cool feature of an electronic keyboard is its portability when compared to other pianos. They can be moved from place to place with ease.
